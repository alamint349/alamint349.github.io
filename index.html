I am a machine learning researcher and software engineer driven by a broad interest in developing intelligent systems for complex scientific and healthcare challenges. My research lies at the intersection of artificial intelligence, deep learning, computational biology, bioinformatics, and medical imaging. I work with graph neural networks, protein language models, transformer architectures, computer vision, multimodal learning, and emerging foundation-model approaches to analyze complex biological and clinical data. My broader research goal is to develop robust, interpretable, and biologically informed AI systems that can advance our understanding of disease, improve diagnostic accuracy, and ultimately contribute to more effective and personalized healthcare.

I hold a B.Sc. in Software Engineering from Daffodil International University, where my undergraduate thesis focused on deep-learning-based colorectal polyp detection. Since then, I have pursued interdisciplinary research across medical AI, computational biology, cancer research, and healthcare machine learning, while also gaining more than three years of industry experience designing scalable software, computer-vision, real-time analytics, and intelligent automation systems. This combination of research and engineering experience enables me to approach scientific problems from both a theoretical and practical perspective — from developing novel learning architectures to building reliable systems capable of addressing real-world challenges.

I am actively seeking Ph.D. and research opportunities where I can contribute to the development of next-generation AI methods for biology and medicine. I am particularly interested in research that combines machine learning with biological knowledge, multimodal data, and domain-specific reasoning to create models that are not only accurate, but also explainable, generalizable, and scientifically meaningful. Through doctoral research, I hope to contribute to the advancement of AI-driven scientific discovery and the development of transformative technologies for precision medicine and clinical decision-making.
