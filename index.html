I am a machine learning researcher and software engineer working at the intersection of deep learning and the life sciences. My research applies graph neural networks, protein language models, and transformer architectures to problems in bioinformatics, cancer subtyping, and medical imaging — with the broader goal of building intelligent systems that improve diagnostics, patient care, and clinical decision-making.

I hold a B.Sc. in Software Engineering from Daffodil International University, where my thesis focused on deep-learning-based colorectal polyp detection. Alongside my research, I bring more than three years of industry experience designing scalable computer-vision and real-time analytics systems.
